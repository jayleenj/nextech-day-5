The Gender Pay Gap


What is the Gender Pay Gap?

The Gender Pay Gap refers to the difference in how much men and women earn on average. Despite popular belief, it does not refer to the difference in pay for doing the same job; it is purely about the average.

Why Does it Matter, and Why Does it Exist?

The Gender Pay Gap matters because it shows how women have historically been undervalued and offered fewer opportunities. It exists today because women have historically had less access to education and career advancement opportunities. While women now have access to more resources, the mindset is still around today, as evident by the gender pay gap. This also means that this mindset could be applied to any community. By caring about and stopping the gender pay gap now, we can stop similar issues from happening to other communities.

How Does this Affect People?

The Gender Pay Gap affects people because it can stop fantastic workers from being truly valued in their communities. Women could be discouraged from following their dreams in regard to occupation, which could eliminate some hardworking people from the pool of applicants. This could result in less competition for higher-level positions, likely giving companies a less-qualified workforce overall.
