The Gender Pay Gap


What is the Gender Pay Gap?

The Gender Pay Gap refers to the difference in how much men and women earn on average. Despite popular belief, it does not refer to the difference in pay for doing the same job; it is purely about the average.

Why Does it Matter, and Why Does it Exist?

The Gender Pay Gap matters because it shows how women have historically been undervalued and offered fewer opportunities. It exists today because women have historically had less access to education and career advancement opportunities while also being expected to stay home and take care of children. Even though women now have access to more resources, the mindset is still around today, as evident by the gender pay gap. This implies that this mindset could be applied to any community. By caring about and stopping the gender pay gap now, we can stop similar issues from happening to other communities.

How Does this Affect People?

  • Causes women to be discouraged from certain careers
  • Eliminates qualified workers from the pool of candidates
  • Companies get less-qualified employees due to a lack of competition

What Does the Solution Look Like?

  • Pay Transparency in Job Listings
  • Increase of Minimum Wage
  • Salary Transparency with Coworkers

How Can I Help?

  • Ask for raises and walk away from unequitable organizations
  • Support female-owned businesses
  • Contact your congress members to pass legislation, such as the Paycheck Fairness Act
